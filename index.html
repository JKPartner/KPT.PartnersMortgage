What is a Pre-Approval?

A pre-approval is a lender's written commitment that you qualify for a loan up to a specific amount. It's stronger than a pre-qualification and shows sellers you're a serious buyer — often the difference between winning and losing a home.

Buying Basics

What 5 Things Drive Your Rate?

Credit score, loan-to-value ratio, loan type (conventional vs FHA), loan size, and property type all influence your rate. Improving your credit score by even 20 points can meaningfully change your monthly payment.

Rate Factors

PMI: What It Is & How to Avoid It

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is required when your down payment is less than 20% on a conventional loan. It protects the lender — not you. Once you reach 20% equity, you can typically request its removal.

Down Payment

Rate Lock: What It Means

A rate lock guarantees your interest rate for a set period (typically 30–60 days) while your loan processes. If rates rise during that time, yours stays the same. The timing of your lock matters — ask Katie when to pull the trigger.

Timing

Closing Costs: What to Expect

Closing costs typically run 2–5% of the loan amount and include lender fees, title insurance, escrow, and prepaid items. In California, some costs are negotiable and sellers can sometimes contribute — your loan estimate will lay out every line item.

Cost Planning

CA First-Time Buyer Programs

California has several programs for first-time buyers including CalHFA (down payment assistance), the Dream For All program, and USDA loans for qualifying areas. Eligibility varies by income, location, and loan type — Katie can identify what you qualify for.

CA Programs

When Does a Refi Make Sense?

A common rule: if you can lower your rate by 0.5–1% or more and plan to stay in the home long enough to recoup closing costs, a refi is worth exploring. The break-even point varies — use our Refi Savings tool to run your own scenario.

Refinancing

What Happens at Closing?

Closing is the final step where you sign loan documents, pay closing costs, and receive your keys. It typically takes 1–2 hours. Katie's team will walk you through every document so nothing is a surprise. Most closings in CA happen through escrow.

Home values have climbed significantly in many California markets. If you've owned for 3+ years, you likely have more equity than you think — which opens doors to cash-out options, PMI removal, or your next purchase.

Summer Is Prime Time to Review Your Homeowner's Insurance

Most homeowners never revisit their insurance after closing. Costs change, home values rise, and coverage gaps appear. A quick annual review can save you money and ensure you're protected — especially heading into fire season in California.
